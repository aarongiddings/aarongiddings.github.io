# Research

My research focuses on combining **computer vision, machine learning, and structured modeling methods** to better understand complex motion and decision-making in basketball.

---

## Action Recognition in Basketball Video

I develop machine learning models that automatically detect and classify basketball actions from broadcast footage. This includes:

- jump shots
- layups
- dunks
- rebounds
- free throws
- screens
- dribbling events

The goal is to support automated statistical analysis and build searchable databases of player actions.

---

## Object Detection and Annotation Pipelines

I design workflows for generating bounding-box annotations and training detection models for:

- players
- referees
- basketball
- basket and backboard

These pipelines support scalable dataset creation for downstream action-recognition tasks.

---

## Scientific Machine Learning

I also work on neural-network approaches for partial differential equations, including:

- Burgers’ equation
- advection–diffusion systems
- physics-informed neural networks
- adaptive sampling strategies

This work connects numerical simulation with machine learning–based surrogate modeling.
